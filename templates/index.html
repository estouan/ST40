Quantum Art

This page presents some of the research work carried out by Estouan Gachelin.

These pages present several methods of using the quantum for art creation.

Quantum music creation

This creation allows users to create music using quantum circuits.

To do this, you first need to choose the number of control qubits and note qubits.

The control bits add quantum randomness to music.

The note bits represent the notes of music that will all be played at the same time.

Then, the user can add quantum gates to change the sound of the music.

The notes played are determined by the note qubit phase.

The CNOT and CRZ doors allow you to change this phase.

To add a gate, you can simply choose a control qubit and a note qubit.

Quantum superposition in images

This work of art is a representation of the quantum superposition.

It is composed of two groups of images, representing classical bits.

Each image represents a quantum state.

The images are placed so that the quantum states are superimposed.

The random transitions were generated using a quantum random sequence.

Music and quantum states

We tried to create music directly related to quantum phenomena.

We used an algorithm called Addition Walk to generate music from states entangled quanta.

The states used are GHZ and W, which are interlocked states.

We generated a large list of music and trained an artificial intelligence to recognize them.

The results show that for long-form songs (100 notes), the AI recognizes them with 75% accuracy.
